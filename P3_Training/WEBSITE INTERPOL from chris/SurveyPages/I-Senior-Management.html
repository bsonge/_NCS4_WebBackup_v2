Sports Security Senior Management Training Course 


This course was first offered at INTERPOL Headquarters in Lyon, France from 12-14 September 2017.  Senior police officials from 27 nations attended the course.  The Sport Security Senior Management Course is a scenario-based practicum of primary areas within the field of sporting event policing and security management:

  1. Decision making
  2. Planning process and incident management
  3. Strategic perspective of strategic, tactical, and operational incident management
  4. Crowd and evacuation management
  5. Crisis information management

The course prepares senior law enforcement officials to use the decision making process to prepare for and respond to a large-scale incident during a major international sporting event. To reinforce the training objectives, participants will utilize skills and knowledge acquired for simulation-supported, scenario-based exercises addressing emergency response by senior-level law enforcement in support of tactical response activities. These activities are designed to hone both individual and team decision making, multi-agency and national coordination, and senior-level incident management and crisis communication skills in the context of major international sporting event operations.
