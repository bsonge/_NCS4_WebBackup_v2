The National Sports Security Laboratory

Scope

The National Center for Spectator Sports Safety and Security (NCS4) at the University of Southern Mississippi has established a national laboratory dedicated to sports safety and Security. The national laboratory has in partnerships with other academic institutions, government agencies, and private sector dedicated to sport security.  The MM Roberts Stadium (football), Reed Green Coliseum (basketball), and Pete Taylor Baseball Park at The University of Southern Mississippi serve as technology sport venues offering opportunities for security observation and practice; technology tests and experimentation; and investigations of feasible, robust security solutions applicable to sport security operations.

Mission of Laboratory

The primary mission of this specialized National Laboratory is to advance global sports security by serving as the Epicenter for innovation, thought leadership, and academic research that generates new concepts and enhances technology, training, and research for the sport and entertainment safety and security industry.

 

Audience:

The University of Southern Mississippi welcomes companies interested in advancing sport and entertainment safety and security technology to consider joining the Sports and Entertainment Technology Alliance.

The Alliance is open to all security integrators, manufacturers, software providers, and other affiliates and stakeholders interested in solving critical issues in the sport safety and security industry.  Affiliate architects, master planners, designers, federal agencies, consultants, and trade alliances are also encouraged to join.
