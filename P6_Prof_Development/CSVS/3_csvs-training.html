What is CSVS training?

The CSVS provides baseline safety and security training for event staff working at sports and entertainment venues. The training was developed by subject matter experts in the sports safety and security industry with grant fudning from the Mississippi Department of Homeland Security. The goal of this training is to equip venue staff with basic knowledge of their roles and associated responsibilities, and to promote standards of behavior that enhance safety and security and the overall guest experience.

How does CSVS help you?

The CSVS is the starting point for training your venue staff. It is pioneering the way frontline event staff are trained in the sports and entertainment industry. This means your venue staff has:

  • Consistency in training and performance for their various roles
  • A clear understanding of the standards of conduct required of them
  • Basic knowledge associated with their position and their important role in safety and security

What role specific modules are available?

The CSVS provides role-specific training for applicants who have been approved by the organization's authorized administrator(s). This standardized, baseline training is compromised of individual modules for 8 staff roles. Hover over the modules below to learn more about them.


Cleaning Attendant Responsible for the cleanliness of the entire property, including the facility, sidewalks, parking lots, and other guest related areas. This includes cleaning the venue before, during, and after an event.
Concessions Serves the patrons food and beverages. They are responsible for managing the consumption of alcohol by those of legal age.
Gate Security Protects people and property in and around the venue. Screens guests for dangerous and prohibited items prior to entry and prevents unauthorized individuals and vehicles from entering the venue area. Also manages crowds during ingress and egress.
Guest Services Representative Answers questions about the venue and provides information and direction to guests. Provides high quality service to guests while making them feel welcome, comfortable, and safe.
Retail Associate Responsible for selling merchandise such as clothing, hats, programs and souvenirs to guests.
Ticket Taker Admits only authorized individuals into the venue who have an appropriate ticket or credential for the event.
Traffic/Parking Lot Attendant Responsible for the safe movement of vehicles entering and leaving the venue site. Also responsible for the safe movement of people crossing active roadways and in the parking lot on the venue site. Training also includes the toll collector who is responsible for processing the parking fee at entrances to the venue site or parking lot.
Usher Directs guests to the correct seating sections. Seats guests and monitors the seating section during the event. The primary source of information about the venue, ushers also handle most guest relations issues to include answering questions, providing directions, explaining venue policies, etc.

CSVS Testing

All modules have a built-in test. Participants must score 100% on their assigned module(s) test in order to receive a certificate of completion. Participants receive immediate feedback on all missed questions. This feedback references course material that participants should review again to successfully achieve a passing score. Once they have reviewed the course materials on the missed questions, the participant has the opportunity to retest on the missed material.

Customizable Training

The CSVS is flexible and adaptable to your training needs. In addition to the standard 8 training modules, your organization’s authorized administrator(s) have the ability to upload customized, venue-specific and workforce development training for their enrolled applicants. You can add your own training products like case studies, videos, PowerPoint, PDF documents, etc. These can be for awareness and familiarization, or test-based. Examples of customized training include:

  • Stadium evacuation course
  • Venue and perimeter layout
  • Local policy and procedures
  • Food safety and alcohol management
  • Sexual harassment
  • Leadership skills
